The HTMLDialogElement.showModal() method opens the dialog over the top of any other dialogs that might be present.

The HTMLDialogElement.show() method of the HTMLDialogElement interface displays the dialog modelessly, i.e. still allowing interaction with content outside of the dialog.

The dialog tags are already in the DOM, but hidden by default. Using the show() or showModal() methods pop them up, and put the "open" attribute in the dialog element. And the close() method does the opposite.

Vanilla modal

This is a simple dialog, with bare minimum styling.

Nothing is needed : auto-focus-trap, aria labels, centering, grey background are already packed with the default behavior, saving few divs and lines of code.

You can close the modal by pressing the close button or with keyboard's escape key.
